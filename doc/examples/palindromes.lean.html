Palindromes

Palindromes are lists that read the same from left to right and from right to left. For example, [a, b, b, a] and [a, h, a] are palindromes.

We use an inductive predicate to specify whether a list is a palindrome or not. Recall that inductive predicates, or inductively defined propositions, are a convenient way to specify functions of type ... ‚Üí Prop.
